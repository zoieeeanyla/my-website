Common Business Crimes

Businesses face many challenges, including crimes that can affect their operations and profits. Below are five common business crimes, explained simply:

  • Fraud: When someone lies to get money or advantages unfairly.
    Read more about fraud here.
  • Embezzlement: When someone steals money or property they were trusted to manage.
    Learn about embezzlement here.
  • Intellectual Property Theft: When someone uses another person's ideas or inventions without permission.
    Details on IP theft here.
  • Cybercrime: Crimes like hacking or online scams, usually involving computers or the internet.
    Cybercrime explained here.
  • Tax Evasion: When people or businesses illegally avoid paying the taxes they owe.
    What is tax evasion? Find out here.

Landmark Business Crime Cases

  • Enron Scandal (2001): A case where a company hid its financial problems to mislead investors.
    Details on the Enron case here.
  • Bernard Madoff Ponzi Scheme (2008): A massive scam where money from new investors was used to pay older ones.
    Learn about the Madoff case here.
  • Volkswagen Emissions Scandal (2015): A case where Volkswagen cheated on tests to show their cars were more eco-friendly than they were.
