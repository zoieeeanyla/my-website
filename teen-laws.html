Important Teen Laws in Georgia

Georgia has specific laws that directly affect teenagers, covering topics like driving, school attendance, and underage drinking. These laws are designed to protect teens and help them develop into responsible adults. Below, you'll find information on three key laws, their implications, and penalties.

1. Curfew Laws

In Georgia, teens under the age of 18 must adhere to curfew laws, which generally restrict them from being out in public or driving late at night. Penalties include fines and notification of parents or guardians.
Learn more about curfew laws here.

2. School Attendance (Compulsory Education)

Teens between the ages of 6 and 16 are required to attend school regularly. Unexcused absences can lead to penalties for both teens and their parents, including fines and court appearances.
Learn more about education laws here.

3. Zero Tolerance for Underage Drinking and Driving

Georgia enforces a zero-tolerance policy for underage drinking and driving. Teens caught with a Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) of 0.02% or higher can face license suspension, fines, and mandatory DUI classes.
